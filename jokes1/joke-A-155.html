"TRAGEDY"

John Kerry was visiting an elementary school in Iowa as part of his campaign tour. As he was introduced to the second grade class, the teacher asked him if he would help the class with their vocabulary lesson. Consenting, he asked the class if anyone one knew the meaning of "tragedy", if anyone could give an example of what a tragedy was.

One of the students raised her hand and said, "Suppose that my two brothers were playing with my dad's rifle and it went off and shot one of my brothers. Would that be a tragedy?"

Kerry said, "That is a good example, but that wouldn't be a tragedy -- rather that would be accident. Can anyone else give an example of what a tragedy is?"

Another student raised his hand and said, "Suppose that on a field trip to the canyon, in our school bus ran off the road and crashed and half of our class was killed. Would that be a tragedy?"

Kerry said, "That is another good example, but that wouldn't be a tragedy -- rather that would be a big loss. Can anyone else give an example of what a tragedy is?"

Finally, one small boy raised his hand and asked, "If you were in your campaign jet and a missle came and hit it and killed everyone on board, would that be a tragedy?"

Kerry said, "Yes, that would. Can you explain why it would be a tragedy?"

"Well," the boy said, "it wouldn't be an accident and it wouldn't be a big loss."

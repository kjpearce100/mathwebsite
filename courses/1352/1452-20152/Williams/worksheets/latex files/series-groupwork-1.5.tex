\begin{questions}


\question Do the following series converge or diverge?  State the 
test you used.  For convergent series with negative terms, indicate if they
 converge conditionally or absolutely.  If appropriate,
find the sum.

\begin{parts}

\part $\displaystyle  \sum_{k=1}^\infty \left(\frac{k+4}{3k-1}\right)^k$

\part $\displaystyle  \sum_{k=2}^\infty \frac{k^3 -3k}{1+ \ln k}$

\part $\displaystyle \sum_{k=3}^\infty \frac{1}{k} - \frac{1}{k+1}$

\part $\displaystyle  \sum_{k=1}^\infty k \frac{3^k}{4^k}$

\part $\displaystyle  \sum_{k=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^k}{2\sqrt{k} +3}$

\part $\displaystyle \sum_{k=2}^\infty \frac{k!}{k 2^k}$

\part $\displaystyle \sum_{k=2}^\infty \frac{3}{k\ln k}$

\part $\displaystyle \sum_{k=2}^\infty \frac{3(-1)^k}{k\ln k}$


\end{parts}

\question
 What is the difference between absolute and conditional convergence?

\question What is the difference between a sequence and a series?

\question Suppose $a_k>0$ and $\displaystyle 
\lim_{k\to\infty}\frac{a_k}{1/k} =\frac {1}{2}$.  Does 
$\displaystyle \sum_{k=0}^\infty
a_k$ converge or diverge?  Why?

\question Suppose $a_k>0$ and $\displaystyle \frac{a_{k+1}}{a_k} = \frac{3}{k}$.
Does $\displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^\infty a_k$ converge or diverge?  Why?

\end{questions}
